Research Web Dev 2024 — Present
Prof. Ike Desmond Odugu — Lake Forest College

IGODO Research Project

Colonial language systems, African educational structures, and the infrastructure of knowledge.

What it is

A faculty-led research initiative at Lake Forest College examining how colonial-era language policies shaped the structure and content of educational systems across Africa. The project synthesizes historical records, linguistic data, and policy documents to map long-term institutional effects.

Why it mattered

Understanding how educational systems were constructed — and by whom, and to what ends — is foundational to any serious policy discussion about learning outcomes in post-colonial contexts. This project contributes original research to that conversation.

What I did

Served as Student Research Assistant: conducted literature review and source analysis, supported data organization and documentation, and independently designed and deployed the project's full public-facing website — a six-page platform with researcher profiles, project dossiers, and a news system.

Recruiter signal

Ability to operate inside a faculty research context, synthesize qualitative and quantitative material, and independently produce a production-grade deliverable outside any job description.

Policy Research 2025 — Present
Evident ABA / Therapy Innovations LLC — Michael Pavlovskyi

BreakingNewsABA Policy Journalism

Translating federal healthcare regulation into publication-ready analysis for a national trade audience.

What it is

An internship at Evident ABA / Therapy Innovations LLC producing policy articles for BreakingNewsABA.com — a trade publication covering the business, regulatory, and clinical dimensions of the applied behavior analysis industry.

Why it mattered

ABA telehealth policy underwent significant regulatory change post-pandemic. CMS CPT code revisions, state-level Medicaid restrictions, and reimbursement shifts created real operational uncertainty for providers. Accurate, timely coverage required understanding both the regulatory mechanism and its downstream business effects.

What I did

Researched and wrote publication-ready articles covering ABA telehealth regulations, CMS CPT code changes, and state Medicaid policy shifts. Synthesized federal guidance documents, state-level policy filings, and industry commentary into structured, accessible analysis for a professional audience.

Recruiter signal

Professional-grade written communication, regulatory literacy, ability to absorb a complex policy domain quickly and produce output that serves a real readership — not a class assignment.
