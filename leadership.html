Leadership

Character
is the
credential.

Technical skills get you into rooms. How you show up in those rooms — how reliable you are, how you handle responsibility, how you treat people when there is no grade on the line — determines what happens next. Leadership is not a section on a resume. It is a track record.

Formally recognized
2025 Lake Forest College Leadership Award

One of Lake Forest College's annual distinctions awarded to students who demonstrate initiative, reliability, and meaningful contribution to campus and community — beyond what any course or requirement asks for.

In practice

What it looks like.

Owning deliverables fully

Every web platform I built for faculty clients — INARELS, IGODO — started with a brief and ended with a deployed, production-grade site. No handoffs, no half-finished work. When someone trusted me with something that mattered to them, I finished it.

Showing up before it is required

Both my professional roles — the research assistantship and the Evident ABA internship — were pursued, not assigned. Nobody told me to build professional experience while carrying a full course load. I decided that was the standard I wanted to hold myself to.

Earning trust through consistency

The 2025 Leadership Award was not a competition I entered. It was a recognition by people who had watched how I worked over time. That kind of trust is built the same way every time: by doing what you said you would do, repeatedly, without needing to be reminded.

Communicating with clarity and purpose

Writing policy articles for BreakingNewsABA is a discipline in saying something real and saying it cleanly. The same standard applies to every client interaction, every project update, every email. Communication is not soft — it is operational.

Community

Campus &
beyond.

Leadership at the college level means more than a title. It means being someone others can count on — and building the kind of relationships that outlast any single semester.

Lake Forest College

Active campus participant — 2023 to present

Engaged across academic, social, and co-curricular dimensions of college life. Built relationships across class years, departments, and student organizations — contributing to a community that extends well beyond any individual course or club.

Fundisky Family Enterprise

Co-founder & operator — family of 10

Co-running a family business with parents and seven siblings while a full-time student demands a different kind of leadership: coordinating across people, holding the long view, and doing the operational work that doesn't come with applause. That is exactly the kind of leadership I find most instructive.

Faculty Research Collaboration

IGODO & INARELS — Prof. Odugu

Working directly with a faculty researcher on a sustained project requires a different discipline than coursework. You represent the project publicly, you manage your own time and scope, and the quality of your output directly reflects on the lab. That is the accountability structure I chose.
